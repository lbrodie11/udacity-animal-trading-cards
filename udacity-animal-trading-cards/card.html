Boston Terrier

The Boston terrier is the first official breed created in the United States. They're nicknamed the “American Gentleman” because of their tuxedo-like markings.

  • Average Size: 10-25 lbs
  • Life span: 13-15 years
  • Temperament: Lively, Intelligent, Friendly
  • Other Name: Boston Bull

The offspring interbred with one or more French Bulldogs, providing the foundation for the Boston Terrier. Bred down in size from fighting dogs of the Bull and Terrier types, the Boston Terrier originally weighed up to 44 pounds (20 kg) (Olde Boston Bulldogge). The breed was first shown in Boston in 1870.
